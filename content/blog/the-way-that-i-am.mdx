There are some things about me that only a few people know.
One of them might sound stupid, but it is the truth.

I have never really lived for myself.

Even now, I still don't. And somehow, I am okay with that. I am even proud of it. This is the way I live. This is the way I work. And all of this started because of one person.

> My mom.

---

When I think about my mom's life, it always feels unreal. Like something from a story, not something that really happened to a person I know.

When she was two years old, her mother passed away. After that, her father remarried and left her behind. She had to live with relatives. People say relatives are family, but sometimes they don't treat you like one.

She was not treated well.

She did not know what love was. She did not know what it meant to be cared for. She learned very early that life was about surviving.

She told me that when she was young, she woke up around five in the morning every day. She worked until it was time for school. After school, she came back and worked again. Washing dishes. Folding big piles of clothes that belonged to everyone in the house. She said the clothes were so many, like a mountain.

Her relatives owned a restaurant, so she helped there too. Not because she wanted to. Because there was no other choice.

She was not paid. She was just a child doing work that was too heavy for her age.

---

When she was around grade 5, she went to live in her father's city. But things did not get better. Her stepmother treated her badly. My mom learned how to stay quiet. How to not complain. How to endure.

By the time she reached grade 10, life became even harder.

In Myanmar, that exam was everything. Other students had tuition classes, good teachers, and family support. My mom could barely afford clothes. She worked all the time just to survive. Subjects like English and physics were very hard for her because she never had proper basics.

Still, she wanted to pass.

She gathered all her courage and asked for help. Her father sent money, but only enough for one subject. Just one.

She knew if she failed, that was the end of her education. No second chance. No one to help her.

But she had no time to study. When she tried, she was scolded for not working. The exam was getting closer, and she felt like she had studied nothing.

So she made a decision.

She stopped sleeping.

She broke her bed so she would not lie down again. She studied at the table. Slept sitting there. She did this for three months. Every night.

Because of that determination, she passed very well in math and Myanmar.

But university was too expensive. She had to stop there.

That was her life. Fighting so hard, and still being forced to stop.

---

There are many more stories like this. Too many. If I write all of them, this blog would never end.

She used to cry at night because of how people treated her. Her childhood was broken. She did not know comfort. She did not know love.

And yet, she gave me everything she never had.

She never compared me with anyone. Never told me I was not good enough. When I failed, she still believed in me. When I was tired of life, she listened. When I was upset because of work, she became my best friend.

When no one believed me, my mom believed me. Always.

---

One day, we were going back home together.

It was a normal day. Nothing special. Then she said something quietly.

She said I was never a burden to her.
She said having me was the biggest blessing in her life.
She said her life was a mess, but I gave her a reason to keep going.
She thanked me for being her son.
She said I was the only good thing in her life.

I am usually a cold person. I don't show emotions much. But at that moment, I had to hold myself so hard not to cry.

I still remember that feeling.

---

Because my mom worked so hard, I can live like this today.

She has worked her whole life. Working became a habit for her. She never takes a day off. Even when she is sick, she still goes to work because of our family's financial problems.

Every time I see that, I hate it. I f\*cking hate it from my heart. Seeing my mom go to work even when she is really sick hurts me in a way I cannot explain.

I don't want her to work anymore. I want her to enjoy life. I want her to see that life is more than just working.

But the more I hate seeing her work, the harder I work myself.

The more I became like her.

In the past three years, I can count the days I didn't work on one hand. I work when I am sick. I work when I am burned out. Sometimes I push myself too far, and I hate working.

But I still do it.

Because I want to give her the life she never had.

I never worked for myself. Everything I do is for one reason only. My mom.

---

I know one day she will be gone.

When I think about that, I feel so lost.
I don't know what my life will look like without her.
I don't even know why I should keep pushing myself if she is not there anymore.

The more I think about it, the more I can't find the answer.

I love my mom more than anything.

I'm not writing this to inspire anyone.
I'm not trying to give lessons.

This is just my story.
This is who I am.

I love you, mom.

I am writing this to honor all the sacrifices you have made for me. ❤️
